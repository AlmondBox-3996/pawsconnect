Most common digestive issues found in Dogs

Digestive issues in dogs can range from minor upset stomachs to life-threatening illnesses. Of course, you’ll always want to look after your dog and ensure as best as you can that your canine friend doesn’t experience any kind of health problems. So, you should be aware of the following five most common dog digestive issues.

1. Digestive System Infections

Getting an infection is one of the most common dog digestive issues. Ways in which dogs can get infections in their digestive tracts include ingesting contaminated water, food, or feces. Bacterial infections can happen when a form of bacteria is ingested and sticks to and irritates the interior of the digestive tract. If the bacteria are in the stomach, vomiting will probably occur. When bacteria are farther down the digestive tract, diarrhea is likely to occur. Viral infections occur when dogs come into contact with sick dogs.

Parasitic infections happen when parasites live inside a dog’s gut. The parasites produce eggs or larvae that pass through the digestive tract and end up in the dog’s feces. Another dog could then eat the feces, enabling the parasite to live on in that dog. Probiotics can be helpful in aiding digestive issues because they break down food in the gut and ensure nutrients are properly absorbed. They can be helpful in many other ways too. 2. Noninfectious Diseases

Dogs can have digestive issues due to noninfectious diseases. Dogs’ digestive tracts are commonly prone to kidney stones, ulcers, and enzyme deficiencies. Such issues become more common as dogs get older.

3. Nutrient Deficiencies

Dogs require a healthy balance of proteins, carbohydrates, fats, vitamins, and minerals to maintain long-term good health. Unfortunately, nutrient deficiencies are a common cause of digestive issues in dogs. That’s sad because nutrient deficiencies only occur when owners do not provide their dogs with food that contains all of the nutrients they need. So, this problem can be simply prevented.

4. Overeating

Overeating is also a common cause of digestive issues in dogs. Again, when a dog owner is responsible, there’s no reason for overeating to happen. But overeating occurs in many dogs, all the same. When dogs overeat, their stomachs can become inflated and hardened. And when overeating turns into a bloat, which involves gas building up in the stomach, a dog can potentially face a life-threatening illness.

5. Swallowing Foreign Objects

Dogs too often swallow objects that they should not swallow, many of which can cause digestive problems. Small objects can pass through a dog’s digestive tract without any major problems, but larger objects can cause blockages in the small intestine and stomach. When such problems are not caught in time, they can lead to serious health issues. Swallowing foreign objects can even lead to fatalities. Common items that dogs swallow that can cause problems include bones, toys, string, fishing hooks, and socks.
